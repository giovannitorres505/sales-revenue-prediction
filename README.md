# Sales Revenue Prediction

## Overview

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on the Amazon Sales Dataset to understand the distribution and relationships between variables, and identify the key factors that influence total revenue.

---

## Dataset

The dataset contains **50,000 orders** with the following columns:

| Column | Description |
|---|---|
| `order_id` | Unique order identifier |
| `order_date` | Date the order was placed |
| `product_id` | Unique product identifier |
| `product_category` | Product category (Books, Electronics, Fashion, etc.) |
| `price` | Original product price |
| `discount_percent` | Discount percentage applied |
| `quantity_sold` | Quantity sold per order |
| `customer_region` | Customer region (Asia, Europe, North America, Middle East) |
| `payment_method` | Payment method used |
| `rating` | Product rating (1.0 to 5.0) |
| `review_count` | Number of reviews |
| `discounted_price` | Price after discount |
| `total_revenue` | Total revenue generated by the order |

## Analysis Steps

**1. Data loading and overview**
Loading the dataset and displaying the first rows to get a quick overview of its structure.

**2. Dataset structure**
Checking data types, number of rows and columns, and missing values per column.

**3. Statistical summary**
Descriptive statistics for numeric columns: mean, standard deviation, minimum, maximum, and quartiles.

**4. Data cleaning**
Handling missing values to ensure data quality throughout the analysis.

**5. Total revenue distribution**
Histogram showing how revenue is distributed across all orders.

**6. Quantity sold analysis**
Distribution and value counts of quantity sold (1 to 5 units per order).

**7. Correlation heatmap**
Pearson correlation between all numeric features to identify which variables are most related to total revenue.

**8. Revenue by customer region**
Boxplot comparing revenue distribution across Asia, Europe, North America, and Middle East.

**9. Discount vs revenue**
Scatter plot showing the relationship between discount percentage and total revenue.

**10. Average rating by product category**
Ranking product categories by average customer rating.

**11. Top products by review count**
Identifying the 10 most reviewed products in the dataset.

**12. Revenue trend by month**
Seasonal analysis of average revenue per month to identify peak sales periods.

---

## Key Findings

- Revenue distribution is approximately uniform, with values ranging from ~$3.84 to ~$2,499.55.
- Quantity sold is evenly distributed across all values (1 to 5), showing no dominant purchase pattern.
- Discount percentage does not show a strong linear relationship with total revenue.
- Revenue is consistent across all customer regions, with no significant regional differences.
- Books have the highest average rating among all product categories.
